**What is imperfect competition?**
The opposite of perfect competition. Whereas in a perfect competition companies are price takers, in an imperfect competition they are price makers.

**Explain the four types of market structure.**
Monopoly. One competitor.
Oligopoly. Few competitors, identical product.
Monopolistic competition. Multiple competitors, similar, but not identical product.
Perfect competition. All corporations are price setters. Alot of competitors.

**Define Nash equilibrium.**
Nash equillibrium is when the individuals, optimizing for some value, reach an equilibrium in decision making, so that every individual maximizes their gain.

**If a group of sellers could form a cartel, what quantity and price would they try to set?
They would try to maximize profits, setting it higher than their marginal cost. They can't sell too high however, because then the consumers wouldn't buy the products.

**Compare the quantity and price of an oligopoly to those of a monopoly.**
Quantity is higher, price is lower.

**Compare the quantity and price of an oligopoly to those of a competitive market.**
Quantity is lower, price is higher.

**How does the number of firms in an oligopoly affect the outcome in its market?**
The higher the amount of firms, the higher the quantity, and lower the price.

**What is the prisoners' dilemma, and what does it have to do with oligopoly?.**
Prisoners' dilemma shows an example of when cooperation would maximize net gains, but at an individual level, it doesn't make sense for participants to cooperate.
