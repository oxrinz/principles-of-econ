**Define natural monopoly. What does the size of a market have to do with whether an industry is a natural monopoly?**
A natural monopoly is an industry where a firm can produce goods at a lower average total cost than the other firms could. If the market is small, then a monopoly arises. However, if the economy of scale curve is decreasing, and there is a large enough market, there may be space for one or more competitors in the market.

**Why is a monopolist's marginal revenue less than the price of its good? Can marginal revenue ever be negative? Explain**
Because monopolists' marginal revenue creates deadweight loss. As a monopolist's prices rise, less and less people are willing to buy the product, creating deadweight loss.

**Give two examples of price discrimination. In each case, explain why the monopolist chooses to follow this business strategy.**
Airplane tickets. A round trip costs less than a one way ticket. This is because the customers of one way tickets are more likely to be business people on business trips, which have a higher willingness to pay for the ticket. By doing this, airlines are able to sell tickets as a price closer to customers' willingness to pay, which differ across the types of customers.
