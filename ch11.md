**Explain what is meant by a good being "excludable". Explain what is meant by a good being "rival". Is pizza excludable? Is it rival?**
Excludable goods are ones that can be prevented from being used on an individual level. For example, ice cream is excludable, because you can simply not give a person ice cream, air on the other hand is not excludable, because you cannot stop a person from breathing air.
Rival goods are ones where the use of a good by one individual takes away from another. Ice cream is a rival good, because there is a finite amount of ice cream in the world.
Pizza is both excludable and rival, therefore it is called a private good.

**Define and give an example of a public good. Can the private market provide this good on its own? Explain.**
A public good is one that is neither excludable nor rival. Air is economically a public good, because the consumption of one doesn't affect others, and because it is impossible to stop a person from breathing air, as noted in the previous answer. The private market cannot provide the air on its on.

**What is the cost-benefit analysis of public goods? Why is it important? Why is it hard?**
Cost-benefit analysis studies the scale of positive externality. It is important for deciding how much of a government sponsered good there should be. It is hard because it doesn't have clear metric that economists can use, unlike in the private market, where sellers and buyers both set prices on how much a good is worth to them.
