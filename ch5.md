**Define the price elasticity of demand and the income elasticity of demand.**
Price elasticity measures how much the quantity decreases as price increases. When the price elasticity is 0, the quanity demanded doesn't change when prices raise, or lower. When the price elasticity is 1, that means the demand increases perfectly proportionally to the price increases. When the price elasticity is over 1, the demand decreases alot as prices change. When the elasticity is less than 1, the market is said to be inelastic. When it is 1, it is said to be unit elastic, and over 1 is called elastic. Elasticity is typically used when trying to figure out which price points would maximize total revenue.
Income elasticity measures how the demand changes as consumer income changes. Since most goods are normal goods, as stated in chapter 4, most goods are said to be elastic. That is, the demand increases as consumer income increases. Inversely, inferior goods are inelastic, as their demand does not increase as consumer income increases, meaning their income elasticity is less than 0.

**List and explain some of the determinants of the price elasticity of demand.**
The amount of supplicants. Tightly packed markets with tons of supplicants tend to be elastic, because a highly priced product can be easier replaced with its supplicants, therefore increasing the elasticity.
Time period being considered. Markets tend to have higher elasticity over longer periods of time, because manufacturers respond to demand with delay.

**Is the price elasticity of supply usually larger in the short run or the long run? Why?**
The manufacturers of products take time to respond to market demand, therefore the elasticity is typically smaller in short periods of time.
