**In what way does the patent system help society solve an externality problem?**
Technological advancements are an externality. As technology (new or old) gets manufactured, there is a chance that the production will lead to new technological advancements that will benefit to the society as a whole. This is a positive externality the companies cannot account for, as they abide by the law of supply and demand. The patent system protects new technological advancements and rewards companies for taking the risk of production, therefore offseting the positive externality.

**What are Pigovian taxes? Why do economists prefer them over regulations as a way to protect the envíronment from pollution?**
Pigovian taxes are an alternative to command-and-control regulations to offset negative externalities. It is a tax imposed on, for example, polution. Companies can pollute as much as they want, as long as they pay the tax.
Economists prefer Pigovian taxes to command-and-control regulations because they are less costly, and set the correct incentives. With a Pigovian tax, every company faces the same tax for pollution, therefore, it forces all companies to adopt less polluting technologies, as opposed to just a few that specialize in anti-pollution, is it would be in the case of command-and-control.
