**Describe the three problems that make the CPI an imperfect measure of the cost of living.**
- Accounting for the rise of quality is difficult and inaccurate at best.
- CPI doesn't account for new goods which raise the quality of life.
- Substitue bias, a raise in price in one product doesn't mean that the quality of life decreases if there are close substitutes.

**If the price of Navy submarine rises, is the consumer index or the GDP deflator affected more? Why?**
GDP deflator, because it accounts for all consumerism, including the government's, while consumer index only concerns consumers.

**Explain the meaning of nominal interest rate and real interest rate. How are they related?**
Nominal interest is the default interest rate, real interest rate is nominal but adjusted for inflation.
