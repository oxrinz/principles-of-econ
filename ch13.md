**What is the relationship between a firm's total revenue, profit and total cost?**
Profit = Total revenue - Total cost

**Give an example of an opportunity cost that an accountant might not count as a cost. Why would the accountant ignore this cost?**
A rejected deal that promised to bring in money. Accountant ignores this cost because it is an implicit cost, it is an opportunity cost, and accountants only consider costs where money flows.

**What is marginal product and what does it mean if it is diminishing?**
Marginal product of any input is the increase of output per unit of input increased. When it is diminishing, it means that the cost of increasing output with that input is less and less efficient as you add more input.

**Define total cost, average total cost, and marginal cost. How are they related?**
Total cost is the total cost of producing x amount of output. Average total cost is total cost divided by units of output. Marginal cost is the cost of how much it costs to raise output by one unit. The average total cost is U shaped, and it's minimum is always where marginal cost and average total cost meet.

**How and why does a firm's average total cost curve differ in the short run and in the long run?**
In the long run, a company can build more factories. Factories can't be built in a day, therefore in the short run companies are limited to the production within the costs and limitations of the factories that it has.

**Define economies of scale and explain why they might arise. Define diseconomies of scale and explain why they might arise.**
Economies of scale is when the average total cost of producing output decreases with the amount of output in the long run. It might arise because it allows specialization among workers, which increases efficiency.
Diseconomies of scale is the opposite, it might arise because the management gets less and less efficient when the company is large.
