**Explain the difference between nominal and real variables, and give two examples of each. According to the principle of monetary neutrality, which variables are affected by changes in the quantity of money?**
Real variables are like purchasing power, they measure wealth. Nominal variables measure the amount of money. Examples of nominal and real version of wage and national GDP. The answer to the last question is nominal variables.

**In what sense is inflation like a tax?**
It reduces the real value of holded money.

**According to the Fisher effect, how does an increase in the inflation rate affect the real interest rate and the nominal interest rate?**
The real interest rate is unchanged, while nominal rate increases 1 to 1.

**If inflation is less than expected, who benefits - debtors or creditors? Explain.**
Creditors, because their money is suddenly more worth than before.
