**Describe what a tariff is, and describe its economic effects.**
A tariff is a tax on imported goods. It leaves producers and the government better off, leaving consumers with higher prices for goods. Tariffs have deadweight losses, the gain of producers and governments does not offset the losses experienced by consumers.

**What is an import quota? Compare its economic effects with those of a tariff.**
Import quota is a limit on how much a nation that import a certain good. It has similar effects as tariffs, with the only difference being that instead of money going into governments, that benefit flows into producers.

**What is the difference between the unilateral and multilateral approaches to achieving free trade?**
Using the unilateral approach a nation opens all trade by itself, without barganing. Multilateral approach means opening up trade while barganing with other countries, often trying to make other countries open trade with you.
