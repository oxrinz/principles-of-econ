What is a competitive market? Briefly describe the types of markets other than perfectly competitive markets.
A competitive market is a market in which are are enough sellers and buyers for each to have a negligible impact on market prices.
Monopoly is a market where a single player is on top, and has near absolute control on the prices.
Oligopoly is when there are multiple competitors which don't compete with each others, each keeping their prices artificially high. An example of an oligopoly is the airline business.
Monopolistically competitive market is when there are multiple players on top, but their products slightly differ from each other, allowing them to indulge in monopolistic practices.

What is a perfectly competitive market?
A perfectly competitive market is where there is an infinite amount of buyers, and an infinite amount of sellers, where any single buyer and seller has no impact on the prices of the market.

Explain normal and inferior goods.
Inferior goods' demand rises when income of the population falls. Normal goods are inverse of that, their demand lowers as income falls. Most products in the world are normal goods. An example of an inferior good is public transportation.
