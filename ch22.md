- Asymmetry of infomration is the difference between consumers' levels of being informed. It shapes consumer choice, and has high impact on the economy
- Political economy is the study of how efficient and how good our governments are at their jobs
- Behavioral economics is the study of human psychology and its effects on economic issues
- Moral hazard is the tendency of a person who is imperfectly monitored to engage in dishonest or otherwise undesirable behavior
- Adverse selection is the tendency for the mix of unobserved attributes to become undesirable from the standpoint of an uninformed party
- The reason that goods only a few weeks or few days old may be sold significantly lower than a brand new product of the same type is adverse selection
- Signals are ways of conveying information about someone's or something's features and capabilities. A diploma gotten from education is an example of a signal. Signals must be costly, otherwise everyone would use them
- Screening is in a way the opposite of signaling. It is when the uninformed party induces the informed party to reveal information
- Arrow's impossibility theorem might be an interesting thing to look into later
- Median voter theorem states that if voters are choosing a point along a line and each voter wants the point closest to his most preferred point, then majority rule will pick the most preferred point of the median voter
- As shown from the ultimatum game, people are willing to sacrifice their own wealth to take away from other, less fair players
